Example Notebook Slides

Use these examples as a guide for what strong engineering notebook slides should show.

Example 1

Problem + Constraints Slide

  • Clear problem statement
  • Criteria for success
  • Constraints and limitations
  • What the design must do
Example 2

Sketch + Decision Slide

  • Two or more labeled concept sketches
  • Notes explaining strengths and weaknesses
  • Chosen design direction
  • Reason for the decision
Example 3

Testing + Revision Slide

  • Photo or screenshot of the test
  • Observations or data
  • What failed or needed improvement
  • Specific change made after testing

Notebook Entry Types

Use these entry types to organize your work throughout a unit or project.

Start

Problem Entry

Define the problem, user needs, criteria, constraints, and what success should look like.

Plan

Idea Entry

Show brainstorming, sketches, concept comparisons, decision matrices, and planning notes.

Build

Progress Entry

Include photos, CAD screenshots, measurements, construction notes, and design changes.

Test

Testing Entry

Record test setup, results, observations, failures, and what the evidence shows.

Improve

Revision Entry

Explain what changed, why it changed, and how the revision improved the design.

Reflect

Final Reflection

Summarize what worked, what failed, what you learned, and what you would do next.

What Strong Evidence Looks Like

Your notebook should prove that you made engineering decisions, not just that you finished a product.

Include

  • Clear dates and titles.
  • Sketches with labels and notes.
  • CAD screenshots showing progress.
  • Photos of prototypes, tests, or failures.
  • Measurements, observations, or data tables.
  • Explanations of design decisions.

Avoid

  • Only showing the final product.
  • Writing vague statements like “we fixed it.”
  • Leaving out failed attempts.
  • Submitting screenshots without explanation.
  • Forgetting criteria and constraints.
  • Turning in work with no reflection.
