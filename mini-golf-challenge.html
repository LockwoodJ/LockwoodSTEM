IED Engineering Challenge

Mini Golf Engineering Challenge

Design, build, test, and improve a playable miniature golf hole that meets real constraints for size, storage, gameplay, and fabrication.

View Challenge Brief View 100-Point Rubric
Project Type Team Design Challenge
Estimated Length 8 Class Periods
Final Product Playable Mini Golf Hole
Total Points 100 Points

Challenge Brief

Your team has been hired to design a portable miniature golf hole for a classroom engineering showcase. Your design must be fun to play, easy to store, structurally sound, and clearly documented through the engineering design process.

Design Goal

  • Create a playable miniature golf hole with a clear start and finish.
  • Use at least one obstacle or interactive feature.
  • Apply sketching, modeling, prototyping, testing, and iteration.
  • Build a final design that is polished, durable, and easy to transport.

Design Constraints

Your solution must meet every required constraint.

Size

  • The assembled hole must fit within a 3 ft × 5 ft footprint.
  • The design must be playable within the classroom space.

Storage

  • The entire hole must store and transport inside an 18 in × 12 in × 6 in copy paper box lid.
  • Parts may be modular, removable, or collapsible.

Gameplay

  • The hole must include at least one obstacle.
  • The ball must have a clear path from start to finish.
  • The challenge should be playable, fair, and repeatable.

FabLab Fabrication

  • 3D printing is allowed for certified students.
  • Laser cutting is allowed for certified students.
  • If you are not certified, submit fabrication requests through the FabLab Fabrication page.
  • Fabricated parts must support your design goals and meet all project constraints.
Go to FabLab Fabrication

Important Fabrication Notes

  • Submit fabrication requests early. Waiting until the end may limit what can be made.
  • Your design should not depend entirely on fabricated parts.
  • All parts should be measured, labeled, and documented in your engineering notebook.
  • Unsafe, oversized, or unclear fabrication requests may be rejected.

Required Deliverables

Each team must submit evidence of both the process and the final solution.

  1. Engineering notebook documentation showing research, sketches, decisions, testing, and iteration.
  2. Initial concept sketches with labels and notes explaining intended gameplay.
  3. Measured layout plan showing the overall size, ball path, obstacle location, and hole placement.
  4. Prototype evidence showing how the design changed based on testing.
  5. Final playable miniature golf hole that meets the project constraints.
  6. Final reflection explaining what worked, what changed, and how the team improved the design.

Suggested Project Timeline

This timeline may be adjusted based on class progress and fabrication needs.

Day 1Challenge introduction, constraints, team planning, and early brainstorming.
Day 2Concept sketches, layout planning, and selection of best design direction.
Day 3Measured design plan, material planning, and fabrication request preparation if needed.
Day 4Prototype construction and early testing of ball path, obstacle, and hole function.
Day 5Iteration and improvement based on testing data and peer feedback.
Day 6Final construction, finishing, and quality checks.
Day 7Gameplay testing, revisions, and engineering notebook completion.
Day 8Final showcase, scoring, reflection, and project submission.

100-Point Rubric

Your grade is based on the design process, final product, documentation, and teamwork.

Category Points Excellent Performance
Design Process 20 Team clearly follows the engineering design process through brainstorming, planning, prototyping, testing, and iteration. Design decisions are thoughtful and supported by evidence.
Meeting Constraints 15 Final design meets the 3 ft × 5 ft footprint, stores within the 18 in × 12 in × 6 in box lid, includes at least one obstacle, and remains playable and safe.
Functionality and Playability 15 Mini golf hole functions reliably, has a clear start and finish, provides an appropriate challenge, and can be played repeatedly without major failure.
Creativity and User Experience 10 Design is creative, engaging, and visually appealing. Obstacles and features improve the player experience rather than simply decorating the hole.
Build Quality and Craftsmanship 10 Final product is sturdy, clean, well-assembled, and carefully finished. Materials are used appropriately and parts fit together well.
Sketches, Layouts, and Technical Planning 10 Sketches and plans are clear, labeled, measured, and useful for understanding the design. Layout shows ball path, obstacles, hole placement, and key dimensions.
Testing and Iteration 10 Team tests the design, identifies problems, gathers feedback, and makes meaningful improvements before the final version.
Engineering Notebook and Reflection 5 Notebook is complete, organized, and shows design thinking. Reflection explains what worked, what changed, and what the team learned.
Teamwork and Professionalism 5 Team uses class time well, shares responsibilities, communicates respectfully, and follows all safety and fabrication expectations.
