How to Use This Site

Follow this workflow when you arrive in class or need help finding materials.

1

Check the agenda first.

The agenda is the source for what we are doing today. Confirm the course, date, lesson title, objectives, announcements, and homework.

2

Open the correct course hub.

Use the IED, POE, or Advanced Manufacturing hub when you need project pages, class resources, course expectations, or design challenge information.

3

Use FabLab pages before making things.

Before requesting fabrication or using tools, review safety expectations, complete required certification, and make sure your file is ready.

4

Use resources when you get stuck.

Use the Fusion tutorials, CAD resources, sketching guides, and engineering notebook supports before asking someone to solve the problem for you.

5

Ask for help with a specific question.

Good help requests include what you are trying to do, what you tried already, what error or problem you see, and where you are stuck.

What to Do If...

Quick directions for common situations.

I was absent.

Open the agenda for the date you missed, read the lesson and homework, then check your course hub for related files or project directions. Ask what must be made up.

Open agenda →

I need to make or print something.

Start with the FabLab workflow. Make sure your file is finished, exported correctly, and submitted through the fabrication request page.

Use the Fusion tutorial library and CAD resources. Try to identify the tool, feature, or workflow that is causing the issue.

Student Habits for Success

Check the agenda daily, keep your engineering notebook current, save your files with clear names, document your design decisions, complete safety training before using equipment, and ask clear questions when you need support.
