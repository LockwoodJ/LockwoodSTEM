POE Units

A full-year student-facing sequence that keeps the normal POE topics while connecting each one to planetary rover systems.

Explore UnitsRover MissionsStudent Resources
Unit 0

Rover Foundations & VEX Systems

Students learn the course mission, VEX platform basics, classroom safety, engineering notebook expectations, and how planetary rovers are engineered for remote exploration.

Major application: VEX Component ID & Ground Support Mini-Build

Open UnitResources
Unit 1

Engineering Systems & Mission Requirements

Students model the rover as a system of systems and convert a mission need into measurable requirements, constraints, and trade studies.

Major application: Mission Concept Review

Open UnitResources
Unit 2

Energy, Power & Rover Endurance

Students analyze work, power, efficiency, batteries, drivetrain choices, and the trade-off between speed, payload, and mission duration.

Major application: Rover Endurance Trial

Open UnitResources
Unit 3

Statics, Chassis Structures & Load Paths

Students apply force analysis and structural design to create a lightweight rover chassis that supports payloads and survives terrain loading.

Major application: Lightweight Rover Chassis Challenge

Open UnitResources
Unit 4

Materials & Manufacturing for Rovers

Students compare materials and manufacturing methods for rover wheels, frames, brackets, sensor mounts, and protective systems.

Major application: Rover Material Selection Study

Open UnitResources
Unit 5

Mechanisms & Sample Collection

Students design mechanisms that transfer motion and force for sample collection, lifting, gripping, and deployment tasks.

Major application: Sample Collection Arm

Open UnitResources
Unit 6

Fluid Power & Actuation

Students use pneumatic systems to model aerospace actuation such as grippers, doors, lifts, and deployment mechanisms.

Major application: Pneumatic Rock Retrieval System

Open UnitResources
Unit 7

Electronics, Sensors & Data Collection

Students build sensor systems for obstacle detection, navigation feedback, environmental data, and mission evidence collection.

Major application: Rover Sensor Package

Open UnitResources
Unit 8

Control Systems & Autonomous Navigation

Students program feedback-based autonomous rover behaviors for terrain navigation and mission execution.

Major application: Autonomous Terrain Navigation

Open UnitResources
Unit 9

Thermal & Environmental Engineering

Students investigate heat, dust, vibration, and environmental protection for motors, batteries, and electronics.

Major application: Rover Survival Design

Open UnitResources
Unit 10

Systems Integration & Mission Readiness

Students integrate subsystems, verify requirements, test the rover, and prepare a NASA-style final design review.

Major application: Mission Readiness Review
