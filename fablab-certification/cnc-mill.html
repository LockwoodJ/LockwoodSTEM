CNC Certification: DMC2 Mini, FoxAlien, and Genmitsu

CNC machines remove material using a spinning cutting tool. Safe CNC work depends on secure workholding, correct zeroing, approved toolpaths, and emergency stop readiness.

What You Need to Learn

CNC setup is the project.

Most CNC problems happen before cutting starts: loose stock, wrong zero, wrong bit, bad toolpath, or clamps in the toolpath. Certification means you can slow down and verify the setup.

  • Secure stock with approved workholding.
  • Select the correct tool and toolpath.
  • Set work zero accurately.
  • Simulate or preview before cutting.
  • Stay ready to pause or stop the machine.

Student Procedure

  1. Check the design, stock size, material, and cutting tool.
  2. Secure the workpiece using the approved workholding method.
  3. Set zero and confirm safe clearance above clamps and fixtures.
  4. Preview the toolpath and get approval before the first cut.
  5. Watch the first motion and first pass carefully.
  6. Stop for chatter, burning, loose stock, bit deflection, or unexpected motion.
