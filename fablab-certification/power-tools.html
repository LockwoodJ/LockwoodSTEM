Power Tool Certification

Power tools save time, but they require focus, guarding, clamping, safe body position, and instructor-approved use.

Equipment You Will Use

Vevor Drill Press

Stationary drilling tool for accurate perpendicular holes.

DeWalt Miter Saw

Power saw for accurate crosscuts and angled cuts under supervision.

DeWalt Driver/Drill

Cordless drill and driver for holes and fasteners.

DeWalt Heat Gun

Heated-air tool for forming, shrinking, softening, and controlled heating.

DeWalt Hot Glue Gun

Adhesive tool for quick fixturing, prototyping, and assembly.

What You Need to Learn

  • Inspect the tool before use.
  • Secure the workpiece before cutting, drilling, heating, or fastening.
  • Keep hands, cords, and loose clothing clear of the tool path.
  • Use guards and safety features correctly.
  • Wait for full stop or cooldown before storing the tool.
Remember: The safest power tool user is not the fastest user. Slow, controlled setup prevents most problems.

Speed & Control Mini-Sim

Soft material: use controlled speed and light pressure.

Student Procedure

  1. Identify the exact tool and confirm it is approved for your task.
  2. Inspect the tool, bit, blade, guard, cord, battery, and workspace.
  3. Clamp or secure the workpiece.
  4. Use the tool with correct body position and full attention.
  5. Wait for the tool to stop or cool before setting it down.
  6. Clean the area and return the tool to its correct location.
