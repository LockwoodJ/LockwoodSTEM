Soldering & Electronics Certification

Learn how to assemble simple circuits, recognize polarity, solder safely, and inspect your work before powering a circuit.

What You Need to Learn

Good soldering is controlled heating.

A strong solder joint forms when the pad and component lead are heated together, then solder flows smoothly onto both surfaces. Rushing usually creates weak joints or bridges.

  • Identify resistors, LEDs, switches, batteries, and polarity markings.
  • Keep the soldering iron in its stand when not actively soldering.
  • Use ventilation or fume extraction.
  • Inspect for solder bridges, cold joints, reversed components, and loose wires.
  • Power the circuit only after inspection.
Treat the soldering iron tip as hot at all times, even when you think it may be off.

Student Procedure

  1. Review the circuit and identify polarity-sensitive parts.
  2. Place components securely before soldering.
  3. Heat the joint, apply solder, and remove the solder before removing the iron.
  4. Inspect each connection for bridges or dull/cold joints.
  5. Clean the station and return tools safely.
