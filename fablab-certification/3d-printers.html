3D Printer Certification: Bambu Lab P1S

Learn how to turn a CAD model into a successful printed part by preparing the file, choosing smart settings, watching the first layer, and removing the part safely.

Equipment You Will Use

Bambu Lab P1S

Enclosed FDM printer used for classroom prototypes and engineering design parts.

What You Need to Learn

Good prints start before the machine starts.

A successful 3D print depends on a clean CAD model, correct orientation, reasonable layer height, appropriate infill, and support only where needed. Your job is to think like an engineer: balance strength, time, material use, and appearance.

  • Export a clean STL or 3MF from CAD.
  • Use the correct printer and filament profile.
  • Preview the sliced toolpath before printing.
  • Watch the first layer for adhesion problems.
  • Remove the part without damaging the plate or printer.

Print Settings Simulator

Student Procedure

  1. Confirm your design meets the assignment requirements and is appropriate for the FabLab.
  2. Slice the file using the approved Bambu Lab P1S profile.
  3. Preview the print and check for floating geometry, extreme overhangs, excessive supports, or unnecessary print time.
  4. Submit or start the print only through the approved classroom process.
  5. Stay for the first layer and stop the job if it begins failing.
  6. Clean the area, remove scrap filament, and document the result.
