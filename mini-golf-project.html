Mini Golf Engineering Challenge

Design a portable miniature golf hole that meets strict constraints while creating an engaging and playable user experience.

Project Overview

In this project, you will apply the engineering design process to create a miniature golf hole. Your work should show clear problem definition, ideation, sketching, prototyping, testing, and revision. The final design should be fun to play, easy to transport, and supported by complete engineering notebook evidence.

Project Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook, design decisions, and final reflection.

Design Process

Problem Solving

Define the problem, identify constraints, generate ideas, select a solution, test, and improve.

User Experience

Playable Design

Create a course element that is fun, fair, and understandable for the player.

Documentation

Notebook Evidence

Show sketches, decisions, photos, test results, and reflection throughout the project.

Criteria and Constraints

Your design must meet the project requirements while showing thoughtful engineering decisions.

  • The hole must fit within the assigned classroom footprint.
  • The design must include at least one obstacle or feature that affects the ball path.
  • The project must be portable and able to be stored when not in use.
  • Materials must be used responsibly and safely.
  • The final design must be tested and improved based on evidence.

Suggested Workflow

Use this sequence to stay organized from the first idea to final submission.

  1. Research and Define: Identify the design problem, criteria, constraints, and user needs.
  2. Ideate: Sketch multiple possible designs and compare their strengths.
  3. Build: Create the course structure and obstacle features.
  4. Test: Play test the hole and collect observations.
  5. Improve: Revise based on testing evidence.
  6. Present: Submit notebook evidence and explain final design choices.

Rubric

Projects are evaluated on process, evidence, technical quality, and reflection.

Problem Definition 15 pts

Clearly identifies the problem, user needs, criteria, and constraints.

Planning and Documentation 20 pts

Notebook shows sketches, design decisions, revisions, and process evidence.

Technical Execution 25 pts

Final design or model is accurate, complete, functional, and carefully made.

Testing and Iteration 20 pts

Uses evidence from testing or feedback to improve the design.

Final Reflection 10 pts

Explains what changed, what worked, and what could be improved.

Professionalism 10 pts

Uses class time, tools, materials, and teamwork responsibly.
